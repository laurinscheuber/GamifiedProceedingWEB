Student Projects in undergraduate Computer Sciences

The Contact Lens Catalogue 2.0 transforms how optometrists access and manage information about lenses and care products. This powerful platform streamlines the search process, offering a comprehensive, up-to-date catalog in multiple languages. With advanced filtering, seamless data management, and user-friendly features, the tool simplifies the day-to-day operations of optometry professionals, making it easier than ever to find the right products and manage customer data.

Initial Situation/Problem Statement

The Contact Lens Catalogue (CLC) 1.0 was developed as a web-based resource aimed at optometry students. While it contains comprehensive technical information about contact lenses and care products, the current version is limited to internal use by students and is not suitable for public deployment. Major functionalities such as master data management, product updates, and customer management are either missing or inadequately developed, making it immature for broader use in the Swiss market.

Project Goal

The primary objective of the Contact Lens Catalogue (CLC) 2.0 project is to develop a robust, user-friendly platform that serves as the central reference for optometrists in Switzerland. The updated catalogue will provide:

  • An exhaustive, up-to-date, and independent source of information on contact lenses and care products available in Switzerland.
  • Comprehensive features to manage master data and streamline product updates, including semi-automated updates for disposable lenses.
  • Support for advanced user management, enabling both student users and external paying subscribers to access and personalize their experience.
  • Enhanced search and filtering capabilities, along with options to save search results and tailor user profiles.
  • Accessibility across multiple devices, including mobile and tablet platforms, ensuring ease of use for all users.

By achieving these goals, the platform aims to become an indispensable tool for optometrists, enhancing efficiency and accuracy in their daily operations while maintaining a high standard of independence and reliability.

Landingpage

A brand-new landing page has been introduced, offering a central hub to explore the platform. It provides a clear overview of key features and is designed to attract and engage new users effectively.

Filters

The platform includes advanced filters for lenses, materials, and care products, enabling users to refine their searches and quickly find the most suitable options.

Multilanguage Support

The CLC is now available in German, French, Italian, and English. This multilingual functionality enhances accessibility for students across Switzerland and supports broader adoption throughout the country.

Developed Solution and its Benefit

The development of the Contact Lens Catalogue 2.0 began from scratch, as the previous version had significant legacy issues and could not meet the fundamentally changed customer requirements. By starting fresh, we ensured the new system would be robust, modern, and scalable to address current and future needs.

  • Automatic parser foundation: Considerable effort went into developing an automatic parser to gather lens product data from manufacturers. However, the parser could not be completed due to a lack of standardized data formats.
  • Clean database migration: The client’s Excel data was successfully transformed into a well-structured SQLite database, ensuring easier data management and improved reliability.
  • Robust API development: A well-documented and scalable API was developed, providing a strong foundation for further feature expansion and integrations.
  • FHNW branding: The platform now incorporates FHNW's official styling, ensuring consistency with institutional branding and enhancing professionalism.
  • Modern implementation: The existing features from the previous project were redeveloped using Java Spring Boot and Nuxt.js, creating a maintainable solution that is ready for handover to an IT firm.
  • Integrated data management: The client can now manage their data directly within the tool, eliminating the need for manual Excel imports.

By reworking the application and addressing the challenges of the previous version, the Contact Lens Catalogue 2.0 is now in a position to be further developed and scaled. The transition to modern frameworks and clean code ensures the platform is ready for future enhancements and professional deployment.

The new implementation not only provides immediate benefits to the client but also establishes a robust infrastructure for long-term growth and sustainability.
